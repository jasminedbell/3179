Exploring the Impact of Smoking Cessation Medicines

Smoking cessation relates to the process of discontinuing tobacco smoking. Tobacco smoking is a highly addictive habit. This is due to the presence of nicotine within tobacco smoke. Nicotine consumption leads to smoking dependence and withdrawals. Within Australia, there are many programs to promote smoking cessation. Notably, prescription medicines are available to treat nicotine dependence. These can be obtained from a local GP.

The following article outlines Australian smoking cessation prescription statistics.

Smoking Cessation Prescriptions (per 100,000 population) by Year and State

This visualisation reveals a decrease in smoking cessation prescriptions in 2022. This decrease appears to have occurred in all states. The largest decrease from 2021-2022 was observed in Queensland. Prior to 2021, prescriptions were constant within states. Across all years, Tasmania had the highest proportion of prescriptions.

Smoking Cessation Prescriptions by Remoteness Area

Major cities contributed the lowest number of prescriptions. This was suprising, given that major cities have higher populations. All regions had a decrease in prescriptions in 2022.

Comparison of Smoking Cessation Medicine Distributions Over Time

Hint: click a column in one visualisation to interact with the other (double click the background to de-select)

From 2013 to 2021, Varenicline was the most dispensed medicine. This was followed by Nicotine, then by Bupropion. In 2022, this distribution changed. Nicotine became the most dispensed medicine. Whilst counter-intuitive, Nicotine is prescribed to relieve discomfort whilst quitting. It works by replacing the Nicotine people could get from cigarettes. Alternatively, Varenicline works to partially stimulate the Nicotine receptor. Whilst both aim to reduce cravings, they work in different ways. Nicotine was more increasingly prescribed due to less side effects. Bupropion was the least prescribed medicine from 2013-2021. This may have been due to its adverse side effects.

Rates of Current Tobacco and E-Cigarette Smokers vs Smoking Cessation Prescriptions

Hint: click a product in the legend to select those values (click the background to de-select)

This visualisation shows the population (%) of current smoking/cessation product users. Tobacco smoking has decreased from 2016-2022. This may account for the decrease in smoking cessation prescriptions. The inverse was seen for e-cigarette use, which increased from 2016-2022. Similarly, tobacco smokers may have moved to e-cigarettes as a cessation method.

Comparing Smoking Cessation Prescriptions Between Age Groups Over Time

Most age groups appear to have decreased smoking cessation prescriptions. Older age groups changed the least in prescription numbers. The youngest age group had the lowest number of prescriptions overall. Almost all age groups declined in prescriptions from 2021-2022.

Comparing Smoking Cessation Prescriptions Between Genders Over Time

For almost all age groups, males held higher prescription numbers. Under 18's and 50-59 had the closest gender balance for prescriptions. 80+ had the highest gender imbalance, with males dominanting in prescription numbers. Almost all age groups improved prescription gender balance in 2022.
